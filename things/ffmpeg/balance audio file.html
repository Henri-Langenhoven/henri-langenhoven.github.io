Using ffmpeg to balance an audio file's volume in three easy steps

Published on 29 December 2025

Step 1

Use volumedetect command to figure out by how much the volume should be adjusted. 

ffmpeg -i file.mp3 -af "volumedetect" -vn -sn -dn -f null -

Step 2

Analyse the histogram_<5>db lines in the output to decide how many decibels result in acceptable clipping.

Generally, a safe amount is the inverse of the max_volume, i.e. about 5 dB in the below example. 

[Parsed_volumedetect_0 @ 000002484fb5dd00] n_samples: 298470
[Parsed_volumedetect_0 @ 000002484fb5dd00] mean_volume: -19.9 dB
[Parsed_volumedetect_0 @ 000002484fb5dd00] max_volume: -5.3 dB
[Parsed_volumedetect_0 @ 000002484fb5dd00] histogram_5db: 44
[Parsed_volumedetect_0 @ 000002484fb5dd00] histogram_6db: 236
[Parsed_volumedetect_0 @ 000002484fb5dd00] histogram_7db: 720

Step 3

Finally, increase the volume with the volume filter command. 

ffmpeg -i file.mp3 -af "volume=5.0dB" file_louder.mp3
