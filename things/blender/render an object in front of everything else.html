Render an object in front of everything else

I had the same problem as this StackExchange post, and the one and only answer at the time of writing suggested making a new scene. I tried that at first, but it seemed to add unnecessary complexity and I got confused. Then I read the comments, and this comment, suggested making a separate view layer instead. I looked up the documentation on view layers, and tried going with that solution. Because the comment didn't include steps though, I was still floundering around, until I looked at the answer again, and happened to see what looked like "view layers" in one of the images. So I investigated the "Compositor," combined the two suggestions, and voilà!

Without further ado, let's crunch the numbers:

1. Make a new "View Layer"

Click the button that looks like a copy icon (highlighted in the image below) top right above the Outliner (the scene view hierarchy thingy).

2. Make the background transparent

This step is non-obvious and probably would have tripped me up, but thankfully I remembered something about transparency in the StackExchange answer. It was in relation to multiple scenes but turns out it's still necessary for view layers. Click on the old-school camera icon in the right sidebar, scroll down to Film, expand it, then check the "Transparent" box.
